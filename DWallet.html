Digital Wallets



The term digital wallet may refer to an electronic device that stores payment information (such as a smartphone) and the program or app used to make the payment (such as Apple Pay, Google Wallet, Samsung Pay, or PayPal). Digital wallets are a contactless payment method that stores your payment options, allowing you to conveniently use your smartphone or smartwatch to make a purchase.

Looking ahead

In a 2020 global study, Mastercard found that 74% of retailers around the world plan to continue to use contactless payments post-pandemic, and current consumer trends suggest that this sustained habit is here to stay. However, one foreseeable speed bump pertains to digital wallets and how most users would have their payment methods centralized on one device. Consumer trust in digital wallets has been fraught from recent hacking incidents, ransomware attacks, and data leaks. In a September 2021 study by The Financial Brand, “43% of consumers didn’t think mobile wallets were secure, and 38% were concerned about losing their device and being unable to make any payments.” There is still a lot that needs to happen before consumers feel entirely comfortable ditching their physical wallets for digital ones, and that tipping point will depend on the implications from the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, future technological advancements in payments privacy and security, and how businesses’ operations adapt to shifting consumer behaviors.
