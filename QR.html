QR codes have become widely popular since the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, particularly in the restaurant industry. There are two main types of QR codes â€“ static and dynamic. Static QR codes canâ€™t be changed once they are created such as email and text codes. While static QR codes contain fixed information, dynamic QR codes can be edited after completion; both the code and content are editable as many times as needed. In addition to providing convenience from a sanitation perspective, QR codes have also allowed restaurants to update their menus, in real-time, as dishes are sold out. This reduces the cost of reprinting menus and the need to tell customers that a dish is unavailable, which have been extremely helpful to restaurants during the pandemic.


There is a myriad of services that allow restaurants to generate a QR code to their restaurant webpage, an online PDF menu, or a checkout payment page. Customers simply need to point their smartphone cameras at it (most smartphones have built-in QR Code readers) and wait for a pop-up notification which when clicked will send you to the webpage. These QR codes are dynamic, meaning restaurants can make any edits to the webpage on their end and those changes will reflect after customers scan the same QR code. This feature allowed businesses to scale their marketing operations by featuring QR codes on television commercials, t-shirts, take-out menus, and other creative collateral. It also enabled restaurants to offer contactless menus on table tents and diners to self-checkout or split the bill straight from their table.


QR codes alleviated restaurant patron pain points, allowing customers to look at menus as soon as they sit down, while they wait for their tables, and place an order without speaking to any restaurant staff. They also do not need a waiter to bring them their check and to process card payments. This ultimately reduces service time variability, maximizing the number of tables restaurants can serve during peak times, and reduces the strain on waiters who are often managing several tables simultaneously. QR codes have simplified restaurant operations in a COVID-19 environment, while allowing patrons to have a safe and enjoyable dining experience.
